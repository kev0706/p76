How Black holes were formed

Primordial black holes are thought to have formed in the early universe, soon after the big bang. Stellar black holes form when the center of a very massive star collapses in upon itself. This collapse also causes a supernova, or an exploding star, that blasts part of the star into space. But this does not explain how the largest black holes have formed. Something must have happened, because the universe is simply not old enough. We will need the largest stars in existence to form these humungous black holes: Quasi stars. Quasi stars are much larger than the current largest stars in the universe. The star must have been so large that the black hole must have been made when the star was formng. A deadly balance could emerge: The gravity fed the black hole so fast, heating it to such a degree that the radiation pressure could have kept the star stable.
